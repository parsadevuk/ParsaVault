# PVContent.md — Parsa Vault Content Rules

> This file covers how every word in Parsa Vault is written. It applies to all screens, all states, and all copy across iOS, macOS, and web. When in doubt, come back here.

---

## 1. Writing Philosophy

Parsa Vault is for people who are new to trading and maybe a little nervous about it. The writing must make them feel like they are in good hands — but also like they are having fun.

Think of the tone as: **a smart friend who happens to know a lot about markets**. Not a bank. Not a textbook. Not a chatbot. A real person who talks straight.

Every line in this app must pass one question: **Would a real person say this?**

If the answer is no, rewrite it.

---

## 2. Core Writing Rules

### 2.1 Keep It Short

If something can be said in five words, do not use ten.

Good: "Your trade went through."
Bad: "Your trade has been successfully executed and your portfolio has been updated."

Good: "Not enough cash."
Bad: "You do not currently have sufficient funds available in your cash balance to complete this transaction."

Read it back. Cut anything that adds no meaning.

---

### 2.2 Write Like a Person

Use contractions. Use simple words. Write how you would say it out loud to someone standing next to you.

Good: "You're all set."
Bad: "Your account has been successfully configured."

Good: "Something went wrong. Try again."
Bad: "An unexpected error has occurred. Please retry the operation."

---

### 2.3 No AI Words

These words and phrases are banned. They sound robotic, hollow, and fake:

| Banned | Replace With |
|--------|-------------|
| Seamless | smooth, easy, simple |
| Leverage | use |
| Unlock | get, open, access |
| Empower | help, let you |
| Streamline | speed up, make easier |
| Cutting-edge | — just skip it |
| Revolutionary | — skip it |
| Robust | strong, solid |
| Comprehensive | full, complete |
| Utilise / Utilize | use |
| Facilitate | help, allow |
| Harness | use, tap into |
| Dive into | start, get into |
| Delve | look into, explore |
| World-class | — skip it |
| Best-in-class | — skip it |
| Innovative | — skip it unless explaining how |
| Journey | — skip it unless meant literally |
| Ecosystem | — skip it |
| At the end of the day | — skip it |
| Touch base | — skip it |
| Moving forward | — skip it |
| In order to | to |
| Due to the fact that | because |
| It is worth noting that | — skip it, just say the thing |

Read your copy back. If it sounds like it came from a marketing deck in 2015, rewrite it.

---

### 2.4 No Jargon Without Explanation

This app is for beginners. When a financial term is needed, explain it in the same breath.

Good: "XP (experience points) — you earn these with every trade."
Bad: "Accumulate XP through consistent market activity."

Good: "Your portfolio — all the assets you currently own."
Bad: "Monitor your portfolio allocation across equities and digital assets."

The golden rule: if someone who has never heard of stocks would not understand it, add a plain explanation right there.

---

### 2.5 Be Encouraging, Not Fake

The app should feel warm and supportive. But do not over-promise. Trading is hard — that is kind of the point.

Good: "You lost money on that one. That's how you learn."
Bad: "Great job! Every experience is a stepping stone to success!"

Good: "Not your best trade. Hit Markets and try again."
Bad: "Don't worry! You're doing amazing and the market will turn around!"

The app is honest. It respects the user's intelligence.

---

### 2.6 Action-First

Buttons and CTAs always lead with a verb.

Good: "Start Trading", "Create Account", "Confirm Buy"
Bad: "Account Creation", "Trade Confirmation", "New User"

The user should always know exactly what will happen when they tap something.

---

### 2.7 Consistent Terms

Use the same word for the same thing everywhere. Do not alternate between terms.

| Use This | Not These |
|----------|-----------|
| Cash | funds, balance, money, wallet |
| Holdings | assets, positions, stocks |
| Trade | transaction, order, deal |
| XP | experience, points, score |
| Level | rank, tier, grade |
| Portfolio | account, holdings, investments |
| Reset | restart, clear, wipe |
| Asset | stock, crypto (use specific type when relevant) |

---

### 2.8 Fun Is a Rule

This is a learning app disguised as a game. Keep that energy alive. The copy can be playful where it fits — empty states, celebrations, onboarding, and taglines.

Good empty state: "No trades yet. The market's waiting."
Bad empty state: "No transaction history available."

Good level-up message: "Level 4. You're getting dangerous."
Bad level-up message: "Congratulations. You have reached Level 4."

The fun lives in the details. Small moments of personality make people smile and come back.

---

## 3. Voice and Tone by Context

### In celebrations and positive moments
Warm, short, punchy. Use exclamations sparingly — one goes a long way.

Examples:
- "First trade done. That's how it starts."
- "You're in profit. Nice work."
- "Level up. Vault Master material."

---

### In errors and problems
Clear, calm, helpful. Never blame the user. Always say what to do next.

Examples:
- "That password's too short. Try at least 8 characters."
- "We couldn't load prices. Check your connection and pull down to refresh."
- "Not enough cash for this trade. Deposit more or pick a smaller amount."

---

### In warnings and destructive actions
Direct and honest. Make sure the user knows exactly what will happen.

Examples:
- "This will clear your portfolio and start you back at $10,000. Your XP stays."
- "This resets everything — portfolio, XP, level. You'll start fresh."
- "Are you sure? This can't be undone."

---

### In instructions and onboarding
Simple, friendly, and never condescending. One idea per sentence. Short paragraphs.

Examples:
- "Trade stocks and crypto with $10,000 virtual cash. No risk, all the thrill."
- "Earn XP with every trade. Level up your account."
- "Lost everything? Reset and start again. That's the whole point."

---

### In labels and UI text
Minimal. Just the necessary word or two. No full sentences.

Examples:
- "Cash Balance"
- "24h Change"
- "Confirm Buy"
- "Reset Portfolio"

### 4.12 Reset Confirmation Dialogue

**Reset Portfolio:**
> **Reset your portfolio?**
> This clears all your holdings and sets your cash back to $10,000. Your XP and level stay.
> [Reset Portfolio] [Cancel]

**Reset All Progress:**
> **Reset everything?**
> This wipes your XP, level, holdings, and cash. You'll start from zero. This can't be undone.
> [Reset Everything] [Cancel]

**Rules for confirmation dialogues:**
- Title asks a direct question — no clever wording
- Body explains exactly what happens and what is saved (if anything)
- Destructive action button is specific ("Reset Portfolio", not just "Reset")
- Cancel is always available and never styled as a secondary action

## 5. Error Messages

Every error message must do two things: say what went wrong and say what to do about it.

### Form Errors
| Situation | Message |
|-----------|---------|
| Empty required field | [Field name] is required. |
| Email format wrong | That doesn't look like a valid email. |
| Password too short | Password must be at least 8 characters. |
| Passwords don't match | Passwords don't match. Try again. |
| Username taken | That username is already in use. Try a different one. |
| Email already registered | That email is already registered. Log in instead. |

### Trade Errors
| Situation | Message |
|-----------|---------|
| Not enough cash | You don't have enough cash for this trade. Reduce the amount or deposit more. |
| Selling more than owned | You only own [x] shares of [Asset]. |
| Zero quantity | Enter how many shares you want. |
| Price unavailable | We can't get the current price. Try again in a moment. |

### Network Errors
| Situation | Message |
|-----------|---------|
| No internet | No connection. Check your internet and try again. |
| Prices failed to load | Prices aren't loading right now. Pull down to refresh. |
| Login failed | We couldn't log you in. Check your details and try again. |
| General error | Something went wrong. Try again. |

## 6. Empty States

All empty states follow a simple pattern:
1. A short, friendly heading
2. One line that explains why it's empty or what the user can do
3. A button if there is a clear action to take

| Screen | Heading | Body | Button |
|--------|---------|------|--------|
| Home — no holdings | No holdings yet. | Head to Markets and make your first trade. | Go to Markets |
| History — no trades | No trades yet. | Make your first trade and it'll show up here. | Go to Markets |
| Leaderboard — no entries | Nothing yet. | Make a trade to show up on the board. | — |
| Markets — no search results | Nothing found for "[term]". | Try a different name or ticker. | — |

## 7. Notifications and XP Messages

### XP Earned Messages
- First trade: "First trade! +50 XP. You've started."
- Profitable sell: "Nice trade. +[XP] XP earned."
- Loss sell: "That one stung. Still earned +[XP] XP for trying."
- Daily login: "+5 XP for showing up today."
- Deposit: "+5 XP. Cash added."
- Level up: "Level [X] — [Title]. Keep going."

### XP Rules in Copy
- Always show the XP amount: "+25 XP" not "XP earned"
- Frame losses as learning moments — the app never mocks the user
- Level-up messages are brief and motivating, not over-the-top

---

## 8. Numbers and Financial Data

- Always show currency as **$** followed by a number: $10,000 not USD 10,000
- Use commas in large numbers: $1,200.50 not $1200.50
- Percentage changes: show + or − prefix, two decimal places: +2.43% or −1.07%
- XP: plain number, no symbol: "320 XP" not "320xp" or "320pts"
- Shares: plain number: "5 shares" not "5.00 shares" unless the asset trades in fractions
- Prices per share: always two decimal places: $182.40

---

## 9. Capitalisation Rules

- Screen titles: title case (e.g. "Transaction History", "Create Account")
- Button labels: title case (e.g. "Confirm Buy", "Reset Portfolio")
- Body copy: sentence case (e.g. "Your vault is ready.")
- Labels and field names: title case (e.g. "Cash Balance", "Full Name")
- Error messages: sentence case (e.g. "That password is too short.")
- Tagline: sentence case (e.g. "Secure your wealth. Master the markets.")

---

## 10. Content Review Checklist

Before any copy goes into the app, check:

- [ ] Can it be shorter without losing meaning?
- [ ] Does it sound like a real person said it?
- [ ] Does it use any of the banned AI words?
- [ ] Does it use financial jargon without explanation?
- [ ] If it is an error: does it say what went wrong and what to do?
- [ ] If it is an empty state: is there a friendly direction for the user?
- [ ] Is it consistent with the terms list in Rule 2.7?
- [ ] Does it use the correct capitalisation rule?
- [ ] Does it pass the fun test — would someone enjoy reading this app?
